# Introduction-to-Cyber-security---Commonwealth-Bank
Platform: Forage (Virtual Work Experience)
Type: Virtual Job Simulation
Cost: Free


What was the objective?

The objective of this virtual work experience was to get a realistic feel for what working in a cybersecurity role at a bank actually looks like day to day. Rather than just learning theory, it put me in the position of someone doing the job and asked me to complete real tasks a cybersecurity analyst might face.

What I did

I completed four tasks as part of the simulation:

Task 1, Identifying Phishing Emails
I was given a set of emails and had to identify which ones were phishing attempts and explain exactly why they were suspicious. This meant looking at things like sender addresses that didn't match the organisation they claimed to be from, urgent language designed to pressure the reader into acting fast, suspicious links, and requests for sensitive information that a legitimate organisation would never ask for over email.

This felt very practical, it's the kind of thing that could realistically land in anyone's inbox, and understanding the signs of a phishing attempt is genuinely useful knowledge.

Task 2, Investigating a Simulated Cybersecurity Incident
I was given a simulated security incident and had to investigate what happened. This involved looking at the details of the incident, working out what type of attack it was, what was affected, and what the likely impact was. It reminded me of the defensive security work I did on TryHackMe, but this time it felt more professional and realistic rather than a learning exercise.

Task 3, Designing a Security Awareness Programme
I had to design a security awareness programme aimed at employees. The idea behind this task was that humans are often the weakest link in an organisation's security, so training staff to recognise threats like phishing is one of the most effective defensive measures a company can put in place. I had to think about what topics to cover, how to deliver it, and how to make it engaging enough that employees would actually pay attention.

Task 4, Building a Basic Security Policy Document
I had to put together a basic security policy document. This covered things like acceptable use of company systems, password requirements, and how employees should handle sensitive data. Writing this made me think about security from a completely different angle, not just technical defences, but the rules and procedures that support them.

What I took away from this

Out of everything I've done so far, this felt the most real-world and professional. TryHackMe teaches you the technical side well, but this put me in the actual position of doing a job, making decisions, justifying them, and thinking about security the way an organisation would. The phishing task in particular was something I genuinely enjoyed because it was about pattern recognition and critical thinking rather than just absorbing information. It also made me realise that a big part of cybersecurity isn't just technical, it's about people, behaviour, and communication.
